**Idea A: "American Heritage Prestige"**

Design Movement: American Federal-era classicism meets contemporary editorial design — think the gravitas of the U.S. Capitol combined with the clean precision of a luxury financial publication.

Core Principles:
1. Authoritative restraint — every element earns its place; nothing decorative without purpose
2. Structured asymmetry — left-anchored text columns balanced by full-bleed imagery on the right
3. Typographic hierarchy as the primary visual engine
4. Warmth through material texture — parchment tones, linen-like backgrounds

Color Philosophy: Deep navy (#1B2A4A) as the commanding primary, paired with antique gold (#C9A84C) for prestige accents, warm ivory (#F7F3EC) as the base, and charcoal (#2C2C2C) for body text. The palette evokes permanence, trust, and institutional authority without coldness.

Layout Paradigm: Asymmetric editorial grid — hero section splits 55/45 with text left-anchored and imagery right. Practice area cards use a staggered masonry approach. Attorney bio section uses a large-format magazine spread layout.

Signature Elements:
1. Thin gold horizontal rules as section dividers
2. Large drop-cap initials in section headings
3. Seal/crest motif echoing the firm's logo as a watermark in hero backgrounds

Interaction Philosophy: Deliberate, unhurried transitions. Hover states reveal gold underlines. Scroll-triggered fade-ins feel like turning pages in a book.

Animation: Subtle parallax on hero imagery. Section headings slide in from left at 0.4s ease-out. Cards lift 4px on hover with a 200ms transition. No spinning or bouncing — everything moves with dignity.

Typography System: Playfair Display (serif) for all headings — conveys classical authority. Source Serif 4 for body text — highly readable, warm. Cormorant Garamond for pull quotes and testimonials — elegant and literary.

**Idea B: "Modern Counsel — Precision & Warmth"** ← SELECTED

Design Movement: Contemporary boutique law — the visual language of a high-end private wealth management firm. Clean, confident, and approachable without sacrificing authority.

Core Principles:
1. Deep navy as the commanding anchor color, used in large structural blocks
2. Champagne/gold as the single accent — used sparingly for maximum impact
3. Generous whitespace as a signal of confidence and clarity
4. Photography-forward — real imagery of families, documents, and professional settings

Color Philosophy: Navy (#1D2B5F) for headers, footers, and primary CTAs. Gold (#B8973A) for accent lines, hover states, and key callouts. Off-white (#FAFAF8) for content sections. Warm gray (#F2F0EB) for alternating section backgrounds. This palette communicates: "We are serious, established, and here for your family."

Layout Paradigm: Full-width sections with contained content columns. Hero uses a dramatic split — dark navy left panel with white headline text, and a full-height photograph on the right. Navigation is sticky with a transparent-to-solid scroll transition. Practice areas use icon-led horizontal cards.

Signature Elements:
1. Thin gold vertical bar as a left accent on section headings
2. The firm's scales-of-justice logo used as a subtle watermark in dark sections
3. Numbered process steps with large gold numerals

Interaction Philosophy: Smooth, professional micro-interactions. Navigation links have a gold underline slide-in on hover. CTA buttons have a subtle fill-sweep animation. Testimonial carousel with smooth crossfade.

Animation: Hero text fades up on load (0.6s). Section content animates in on scroll with a gentle upward drift. Practice area cards have a gold left-border reveal on hover. Smooth scroll throughout.

Typography System: Cormorant Garamond (serif) for hero and section headings — elegant, classical, authoritative. Lato (sans-serif) for navigation, body text, and UI elements — clean and highly readable. This pairing balances tradition with modernity.

**Idea C: "Virginia Verdant — Land & Legacy"**

Design Movement: Southern Tidewater meets contemporary sustainability design — the visual warmth of Virginia's landscape (rolling hills, Potomac River, colonial architecture) expressed through a modern lens.

Core Principles:
1. Forest green as the primary anchor — evokes permanence, growth, and the Virginia landscape
2. Cream and warm white for breathing room
3. Copper/bronze accents for warmth and distinction
4. Organic asymmetry — layouts that feel grown rather than built

Color Philosophy: Deep forest green (#1B3A2F) as primary. Copper (#A0522D) as accent. Cream (#FDF8F0) as base. This palette says: "Rooted in Virginia, built to last generations."

Layout Paradigm: Organic grid with diagonal section breaks. Hero uses a full-bleed landscape photograph with a large serif headline overlaid. Sections break at 8-degree angles using CSS clip-path. Attorney bio uses a circular portrait with text wrapping.

Signature Elements:
1. Leaf/laurel motifs (echoing the firm logo's wreath) as decorative dividers
2. Diagonal section breaks creating visual momentum
3. Copper horizontal rules and accent borders

Interaction Philosophy: Warm, organic transitions. Hover states bloom outward like opening a flower. Scroll reveals feel like unfolding a document.

Animation: Diagonal wipe transitions between sections. Cards expand slightly on hover. Testimonials slide in from alternating sides.

Typography System: EB Garamond for headings — classical Virginia sensibility. Nunito Sans for body — friendly and approachable.
